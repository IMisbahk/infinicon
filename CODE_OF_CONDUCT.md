# Code of Conduct

Infinicon is an open-source infrastructure project. Contributions should be rigorous, direct, and respectful.

## Expected Behavior

- Challenge ideas, not people.
- Explain technical trade-offs clearly.
- Assume good intent while still requiring evidence.
- Keep discussions focused on project goals.
- Make space for contributors with different levels of experience.

## Unacceptable Behavior

- Harassment, threats, or personal attacks.
- Dismissive comments based on identity or background.
- Repeatedly derailing technical discussion.
- Sharing private information without permission.
- Bad-faith contributions meant to waste maintainer time.

## Technical Disagreement

Strong disagreement is welcome when it improves the project.

Good disagreement includes:

- Clear reasoning.
- Alternatives.
- Trade-offs.
- Evidence from specs, tests, or production experience.

Bad disagreement relies on status, insults, or vague certainty.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may remove comments, close issues, reject contributions, or limit participation when behavior harms the project.

Enforcement should be proportionate, documented when appropriate, and focused on protecting a serious engineering environment.
